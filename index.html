Alan Turing

"Many of us in Hut 8 felt that the magnitude of Turing's contribution was never fully realised by the outside world." - Hugh Alexander

Alan Turing was born on June 23, 1912, in Maida Vale, London.

At a young age, Turing already showed signs of the genius he was to later display more prominently.

In 1926, at age 13, Turing attended Sherborne School in Sherborne, Dorset.

Turing was more naturally inclined towards math and science despite his school's emphasis on the classics.

At Sherborne, Turing formed a significant relationship with Christopher Morcom, described as his 'first love'.

Turing studied as an undergraduate from 1931 to 1934 at King's College, Cambridge, graduating with a first-class honours in Mathematics.
In 1936, Turing published a paper in which he reformulated and replaced Kurt GÃ¶del's results with the formal and simple hypothetical devices that became known as Turing machines.
Turing proved that his "universal computing machine" would be capable of performing any conceivable mathematical computation if it were representable as an algorithm.
The central concept of the modern computer was due to Turing's paper. To this day, Turing machines are a central object of study in theory of computation.

From 1936 to 1938, Turing studied under Alonzo Church at Princeton University, obtaining his PhD in 1938.

During the 2nd world war, Turing was one of the main codebreakers of German ciphers at Bletchley Park.

Within weeks of arriving at Bletchley, with some help, Turing built the bombe to help decode the German Enigma machine.

The combined efforts of Turing and everyone at Bletchley shortened WW2 by a few years, saving millions of lives.

After the war, Turing continued his work on computers and mathematical biology. He later proposed the Turing test, an attempt to define a standard for a machine to be called 'intelligent'.
Turing was convicted for indecency in 1952 and later died of cyanide poisoning in 1954. The Imitation Game is a 2014 film depicting his service at Bletchley and snippets of his life.
